# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two overlapping audiences, served simultaneously:

**Competitive OSM players** — experienced tacticians who know the game, want an edge, and treat the anti-tactic finder and squad analyzer as real decision tools. They arrive with a specific opponent formation in mind and want the answer fast.

**Casual players** — less confident with formations and mentalities, using the site as a trusted guide. They respond to authority, clear language, and curated recommendations.

Both land on the same surface. The brand must feel credible enough for veterans and approachable enough for newcomers. The tools do the heavy lifting for both.

## Product Purpose

OSM NEXT LEVEL is a tactical intelligence platform for Online Soccer Manager players. Built and curated by omerovvvvv, it provides formation counter-recommendations, squad value analysis, weekly meta reports, daily tactic picks, and a premium tier with tournament-specific strategies.

Success looks like: a player selects their opponent's formation and conditions, receives a confident counter-tactic recommendation, and trusts it enough to use it in a real match.

## Brand Personality

**Authoritative · Tactical/sharp · Energetic/electric · Premium/exclusive**

Voice: A seasoned coach who has seen every formation. Confident, direct, no hedging. Speaks in decisions, not options. Energy of a match-day brief, not a forum post.

Emotional goal: The user should feel like they have insider knowledge — that they are better prepared than their opponent.

## Anti-references

- **Generic gaming forums (Reddit-dark)**: Crowded, no hierarchy, user-generated clutter, zero brand. OSM NL has a distinct editorial voice.
- **SaaS marketing cliché**: Cream/sand backgrounds, identical rounded card grids, gradient text on every heading, hero-metric templates, small-caps eyebrows above every section. Common AI-scaffold patterns to reject.
- **Betting/odds sites**: Aggressive promos, low-trust visual language, neon overload without restraint.
- **Plain football stats dashboards**: Pure data, no personality, spreadsheet aesthetics. The tools here have character.

## Design Principles

1. **Decisions, not options.** Every screen should lead the user toward one confident answer, not present equal choices. Hierarchy is opinionated.
2. **Match-day urgency without noise.** Energy comes from sharp type, deliberate motion, and purposeful contrast — not from shouting with color or scale.
3. **Authority through restraint.** Premium feel is earned by precision (spacing, alignment, copy), not decoration (gradients, glass, blur stacks).
4. **Tools feel like tools.** The finder and analyzer should feel immediate and tactile — fast feedback, clear state, no fluff between input and result.
5. **Multilingual-first.** TR/EN/PT/HU/AR audiences are equal. Layout, rhythm, and hierarchy must hold across all scripts including RTL Arabic.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target: WCAG AA (4.5:1 body text, 3:1 large text and UI components)
- Reduced-motion: all animations must have `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)` alternatives
- RTL: Arabic layout fully supported via `dir="rtl"` — spacings, alignment, and transitions must flip correctly
- Keyboard navigation: interactive tool controls (formation selectors, location toggles, strength buttons) must be fully keyboard-accessible
